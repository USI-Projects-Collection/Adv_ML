\subsection{Results}
Our results broadly support the paper's central claim: adding register tokens to Vision Transformers consistently improves unsupervised object discovery performance, reduces high-norm outlier tokens, and produces cleaner attention patterns. The register improvement holds across all three model families (DINOv2, OpenCLIP, DeiT-III), all three evaluation datasets, and all qualitative experiments. However, absolute CorLoc numbers are systematically 15-20\% points below the paper's.

\subsubsubsection{\textbf{Norm distributions.}} 
Figure~\ref{fig:token_norms_dinov2} reproduces the paper's key qualitative finding: without registers, patch tokens have a bimodal norm distribution with a high-norm tail alongside normal patches. With 4 registers, this tail disappears — patch norms become uniform — and the register tokens themselves sit at distinct, elevated norm levels. This directly supports the claim that registers absorb the high-norm outlier content.
